Syrian Refugee Crisis

The Syrian Refugee Crisis is a result of the civil war that is going on in the Syrian Arab Republic. The War is an ongoing multi-sided armed conflict in Syria fought primarily between the Syrian Arab Republic led by President Bashar al-Assad, along with its allies, and various forces opposing both the government and each other in varying combinations.

The worst sufferers as a result of this war are the common people in Syria. The war has resulted into a death toll of more than 400,000 since 2011, according to the World Bank, and 5 million people are seeking refuge abroad and over 6 million have been displaced internally, according to the UN agencies. By June 2017, the UN also estimated that 540,000 people were still living in besieged areas.

UNHCR is a United Nations agency that works for protecting and assisting refugees around the world. They also maintain all the refugee data. As of 2015, there are more than 11.5 million Syrian persons of concern according to the UNHCR data. The below dashboard has been built to shed light on the situation of the refugees in the country, and to understand the the refugee crisis in more detail. One can draw many inferences from the dashboard and find out the details of as to why the situation has been changing as seen in the data. The data has been represented for five years before the war and five years during the war to understand the dynamic better.

Before reading the dashboard there are various rules one needs to know about the data collection which can be also found out on the UNHCR website for the data.

  • Persons of concern in the 'time-series' dataset are categorized according to the situation they are in. You can read about it in detail here
  • The data is completely available until 2015. The 2016 data is sometimes marked with asterisks for anonymity. There is no data available after 2016. Hence the dashboard uses data only till 2015.
  • The demographic data is not always available, hence the data in the 'demographics' dataset cannot be reconciled with 'time-series' data. The data for Syrian Arab Republic for 2009 is completely unavailable
